João Pedro Pedroso is an associate professor at the Faculty of Sciences, University of Porto (FCUP), and a researcher at CMUP, the Centre for Mathematics of the University of Porto. His work focuses on the intersection of mathematical programming, heuristic search, and data-driven decision-making. He collaborated in the development of PySCIPOpt, the Python interface for the SCIP optimization suite, and is a co-author of the book "Mathematical Optimization: Solving Problems using Python and Gurobi" (published in Japanese). These works have contributed to bridging the gap between high-level modeling and high-performance solvers for both students and practitioners. He currently directs GEMAC, FCUP's office dedicated to the interface between academia and societal users of statistics and mathematical modeling.
